# Creator Withdrawals

Unlike the instance administrator, **Tessera NEVER stores the private keys of content creators.**
PeerTube is a decentralized, multi-creator platform, so the withdrawal of video earnings operates using a non-custodial model based on **EIP-712**.

## The Withdrawal Flow (BurnIntent)

For a creator to withdraw the earnings generated by their videos, they must follow this decentralized process:

1. **Link Wallet**: The creator navigates to their channel settings in PeerTube and saves their public address (e.g., `0xABC123...`).
2. **Check Balance**: The PeerTube plugin queries `GET /api/connectors/peertube/creator/balance?address=0xABC...` to display the available funds to the creator.
3. **Request Withdrawal**: When the creator wishes to cash out, they click "Withdraw".
4. **Sign with MetaMask (EIP-712)**:
   - The plugin calls `POST /creator/prepare-withdraw`.
   - Tessera creates a document called a `BurnIntent` with the exact withdrawal amount.
   - The creator will see a MetaMask popup asking them to **cryptographically sign** this text document. This step is completely free and consumes no "gas" (requires no network balance).
5. **On-Chain Execution**:
   - Once the creator signs the message, the plugin sends the encrypted signature to `POST /creator/complete-withdraw`.
   - Tessera verifies that the signature cryptographically matches the original creator, and then, acting as the network operator, executes the withdrawal to the creator's personal wallet on the Arc network.

Thanks to this model, every creator maintains total control over their earnings, ensuring that neither the PeerTube administrator nor the Tessera server can access their funds without their digital signature.
